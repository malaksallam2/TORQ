// 01 — Overview

What is TORQ?

A fully autonomous forklift robot designed for warehouse environments — built to navigate, locate, and lift pallets without human intervention.

The Problem

Modern warehouses are pushing upward to save floor space — but conventional forklifts can't keep up. Bulky, slow, and operator-dependent, they struggle in tight aisles and drive up costs through energy waste, idle time, and human error.

Our Solution

TORQ is a compact, fully autonomous forklift that moves in any direction, lifts with precision, and navigates complex warehouse environments without a human at the controls — cutting costs, saving space, and keeping operations running.

Mecanum Mobility

Four mecanum wheels allow TORQ to translate in any direction — forward, sideways, and diagonally — without rotating the chassis, making it ideal for tight warehouse aisles.

Computer Vision

An onboard camera detects ArUco fiducial markers on pallets, computing real-time pose estimation to guide precise alignment and autonomous docking.

// 02 — Key Systems

System architecture

Four core subsystems working in concert to deliver fully autonomous pallet handling.

01

Mecanum Drive

Four independently driven wheels with angled rollers produce omnidirectional motion — enabling lateral slides and diagonal paths with zero turning radius.

02

Winch Lifter

A motorised winch system raises and lowers the forks with precision, handling vertical load while keeping the platform centre of mass stable throughout the lift cycle.

03

ArUco Vision

Onboard camera detects printed ArUco markers on pallets. Pose estimation algorithms extract real-time position and orientation for precise docking maneuvers.

04

Autonomous Control

Sensor fusion ties navigation, vision, and actuation together — TORQ plans its path, approaches the target, docks, lifts, and transports without any operator input.

05

Seamless Mode Switching

Switch instantly between fully autonomous and manual teleoperation — ideal for edge-case overrides, setup, and maintenance. Transitions are smooth and state-preserving, requiring no restart or re-initialisation.
